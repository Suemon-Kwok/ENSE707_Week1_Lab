1. Why does regular committing support software quality?

Frequent commits create small, reviewable checkpoints: easier to spot when a defect was introduced, easier to roll back, and it forces you to build/test in small increments rather than one big risky change.



2. How can Git history provide evidence of individual progress?

Commit timestamps, messages, and diffs (via git log, git blame) show who wrote what and when: useful evidence in a team project for individual contribution.
